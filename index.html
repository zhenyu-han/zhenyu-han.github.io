Research

Synthesis and Biological Testing of Quinone Products

Many organic compounds found in plants and micro-organisms have been found to be useful in therapeutic applications. A wide range of natural products have been reported to have significant inhibitory activity vs certain enzymes. Working with Dr. Lashley and collaborators in University of Frankfurt, Germany, we aim to develop new synthetic pathways for targed quinone derivatives and to test their anticancer activities.

Modeling Mitochondria Activities in Alzheimer’s Disease Progression

Alzheimer’s disease (AD) is a neurodegenerative disease that is characterized by the accumulation of neurofibrillary tangles and Amyloid beta plaques. The effects of tangles and plaques on mitochondrial viability is not fully understood, but the theoretical effects can be separated into affecting three areas of mitochondrial function: productivity, maintenance, and proliferation. Working with Dr. Coleman, our goal is to investigate changes in signaling and chemical gradients related to mitochondrial productivity and maintenance, and how responses to perturbations can be modulated. This offers a novel, concise way of investigating specific mitochondrial activities related to gradients and signaling fluctuations.
